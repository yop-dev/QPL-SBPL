## Company (Quezon Power / QPL)

Quezon Power (Philippines), Limited Co. owns and operates the 460 MW net coal-fired
electric generating facility and the 31-kilometer transmission line in Barangay Cagsiay 1,
municipality of Mauban, Quezon. It commenced commercial operations in May 2000 and supplies
power to the Luzon grid under a 25-year agreement with Manila Electric Co. (Meralco).

It is the first Build-Own-Operate power project in the Philippines and the first power project
in the country undertaken without a sovereign guarantee. The parent company is Electricity
Generating Public Co. (EGCO), Thailand's first independent power producer, established in 1992.

**Taglines / positioning:**
- "Through its Mauban-based power facility, Quezon Power contributes to nation-building by
  providing stable, reliable, and competitively-priced electricity to the Luzon grid."
- "We go beyond generating power."

## Asset — The Power Plant (technical specs)

The Quezon Power Project is the first base-load IPP that sells electricity to a non-government
utility in Southeast Asia, and the first to privately own a high voltage transmission line.

- 460 MW net pulverized coal-fired plant, GE steam turbine/generator for base-load operation
- Foster Wheeler boiler with natural circulation
- Coal handling: BMH Siwertell ship unloader, Italimpianti stacker/reclaimer, Penn crusher
- Once-through seawater cooling system
- Low NOx burners (< 200 ppm capability)
- Upgraded electrostatic precipitator with wet scrubber FGD system
- Continuous emission monitoring (O2, SO2, NOx, opacity/particulate)
- PI / Eta Pro performance data acquisition system
- ABB Bailey Infi 90 DCS and GE Speedtronic Mark V controls

## The Three Companies (org structure)

**QPL — Quezon Power (Philippines), Limited Co.** — Owner/operator.

**QMSI — Quezon Management Services Inc. (Philippine Branch Office)** — Administrative arm.
Administers key commercial contracts (power purchase agreement, O&M contract, coal procurement,
diesel oil and fly ash contracts); accounting and tax; reporting, budgeting and business planning;
financing; insurance procurement. Oversees Public/Community Relations programs (health, education,
livelihood, environment, road safety) and HSE / permit management and transmission line management.

**PEPOI — Pearl Energy Philippines Operating, Inc.** — Operator of the plant, with emphasis on
operational excellence and continuous improvement. Handles staffing/safety/security, accounting
and budgeting for O&M, procurement and engineering CAPEX, coal inventory and ash pile oversight,
and environmental monitoring via its Environmental Management System Compliance Program (EMSCOP),
a first in the Philippines.

## Corporate Social Responsibility (Sustainable Development Program)

**Education** — College scholarships for deserving high school students; support for out-of-school
youth in technical/vocational training; teacher enhancement courses.

**Health** — Medical and dental services to the municipality's 40 barangays with health lectures;
feeding program for malnourished children in two elementary schools plus parent nutrition education.

**Livelihood** — Entrepreneurial and leadership skills training for farmers, fisher folk, women,
and youth. Granted funds to ABS-CBN Bayan Foundation for microentrepreneur loans (100% repayment).

**Environment** — ESP, continuous emissions monitoring system (CEMS), waste water treatment
facilities, covered conveyor belt. Monitoring of water quality, marine ecology, agriculture,
and terrestrial ecosystems.

**Host Community Development (DOE ER 1-94)** — Roads and pathways, bridges, spillways and
irrigation systems, water systems, public markets, schools and day care centers.
